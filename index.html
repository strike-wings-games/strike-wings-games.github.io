THE PREMISE

THE LONGVIGIL

Humanity broke out of Sol, fought two long wars against her own runaway daughters, and buried her dead by the million. Earth drew inward. Her churches became her government. Mars, Earth’s first colony and her heaviest industrial base, refused to follow.

In 2188 Mars broke from the Alliance and named itself after the insult Earth used to dismiss it: the Stepping Stone. The war that followed is the war you fly in.

POWERS OF SOL

THREE FLAGS.NO TRUCE.

Two flags fight the war. A third was built to profit from it, and both sides keep it in business.

FIRST POWER · EARTH

THE TERRAN ALLIANCE

“The sword is holy.”

Earth fought two unwinnable wars against her own runaway daughters, buried a century of dead, and drew inward. Across sixty years (the Templar Turn, 2120 to 2180) a traumatised republic drifted, one reasonable accommodation at a time, into a theocracy: the Aethon Covenant, six ecclesiastical Orders, a Synod where the navy used to be. Eleven billion souls now launch warships only after a chaplain raises the monstrance. Green hull, red cross, and the finest kinetic weapons in known space.

SECOND POWER · MARS

THE STEPPING STONE ALLIANCE

“That which is built is sacred.”

Mars built most of Earth’s fleet and refused Earth’s faith. To a workers’ democracy of welders and engineers, priests at the gunnery board were simply insane. When Earth made the Covenant a condition of trade, Mars broke away in 2188 and took the slur Earth used to dismiss it, the Stepping Stone, as its name. Pell-pattern shields under open Halcyon licence, yards that launch a warship a day, grey Martian steel. The Reaper is theirs; so is the future, if they hold.

THIRD POWER · THE CHARTER

THE CONSORTIUM

“The bid that prints.”

The third power neither flag will admit is a power. It began with the Tharsis Mutiny of 2167, when three captains refused to fire on civilian rescue traffic, were bought out, and walked off Mars aboard a retired dreadnaught. Today it is a chartered mercenary state of forty-two Wings under Concord law. The seventeen-year war breeds a backlog of work line crews won’t touch: contested-corridor escort, asset retrieval, deniable strikes, capital recovery. The Consortium clears it for cash. No flag, no sectarian preference, a contract from the Iron Lamb and its counter from the SSA in the same week. Both navies hire it; both resent it; neither can afford to dissolve it. Its sodium-yellow plume is a warning and a brand at once: we want you to see us coming.

THE FRONTIER

THE WAR LIVESAT THE EDGES

Patrol Outpost Saint Gabriel, 82 Eridani outer system. One Talon squadron, a repair slip, and a sign that asks every pilot to speed the word. Neither fleet can land on the other’s homeworld and hold it, so the war is fought at places nobody on either world had heard of until the casualty lists arrived.
